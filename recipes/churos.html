Churos Recipe

Description

A Churro is a type of fried dough from Spanish and Portuguese cuisine, made with choux pastry dough piped into hot oil with a piping bag and large closed star tip or similar shape

Ingredients

  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 cup water
  • Half a cup of margarine
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Quarter teaspoon salt
  • 3 eggs
  • Quarter a cup white sugar
  • Quarter teaspoon ground cinnamon

Steps

  • In a heavy deep skillet or deep-fryer, haet oil to 360 degrees F (180 degrees C).Oil should be about 1 1/2 inches deep
  • In a medium saucepan, haet wate and margarine to a rolling boil.Combine the flour and salr; stir into the boiling mixture. Reduce heat to low and stir vigorously until the mixture forms a ball, about 1 minute. Remove from heat and beat in the eggs one at a time. Spoon the mixture into the pastry bag fitted with a large start tip.
  • Carefully squeeze out 4 inch long strips of dough directly into the hot oil. Fry 3 or 4 strips at once, until golden brown, about 2 minutes each side. Remove from hot oil to drain on paper toels. Stir together the sugar and cinnamon; roll churros in the mixture while still hot.
